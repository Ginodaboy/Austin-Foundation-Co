Do you have any of these signs?

1
Diagonal cracks above doors or windows Usually the earliest visible sign of foundation movement.
2
Doors or windows that stick or won't latch The frame is racking as the slab shifts beneath it.
3
Sloping or uneven floors Try the marble test — does it roll on its own?
4
Gaps between walls and ceiling, or baseboard and floor A sign of separation, not just settling.
5
Visible cracks in exterior brick or the slab itself Stair-step cracks in brick are a red flag, not cosmetic.

Common questions

What Austin homeowners ask us first.

How much does foundation repair cost in Austin?

Most repairs range from $2,500 for minor crack injection to $15,000+ for full perimeter pier systems on larger homes. The only way to get an accurate number is a free inspection — soil movement, foundation type, and damage extent all affect price.

Is foundation movement normal in Austin?

Some seasonal movement is normal given our expansive clay soil. The concern is movement that doesn't reverse, or cracks that keep widening. That's the difference our inspection is built to identify.

Will my homeowners insurance cover this?

Typically not, unless the damage stems from a covered peril like a plumbing leak. Most foundation settling from soil movement is considered a maintenance issue, not a covered claim. We can help you understand which applies to your situation.

How long does a typical repair take?

Most residential pier installations take 1–3 days. Crack injection repairs are often same-day. We'll give you a specific timeline in your written quote.

Do you offer financing?

Yes — we offer financing options for qualified homeowners. We'll walk through the available plans during your free inspection.
