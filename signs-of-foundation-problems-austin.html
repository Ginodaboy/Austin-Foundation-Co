10 Signs of Foundation Problems in Austin Homes

From the earliest hairline crack to the clearest structural red flag — what each sign actually means, and how urgently it's worth acting on.

Updated for 2026 6 min read Reviewed by our inspection team

Foundation problems rarely announce themselves all at once. They show up gradually, as a handful of small, easy-to-explain-away symptoms — until enough of them stack up that it's obviously not "just an old house." Here's the full list, roughly in the order they tend to appear, with an honest read on how urgent each one is.

01
Early sign

Diagonal cracks above doors and windows

Usually the very first visible sign. These radiate at roughly 45 degrees from a corner of the frame, as the structure settles unevenly above it.

02
Early sign

Hairline cracks in drywall

Thin, stable cracks that aren't growing are usually cosmetic — but worth a mental note, since they're often the first thing you'll notice before anything else on this list shows up.

03
Moderate

Doors or windows that stick or won't latch

The frame is racking out of square as the foundation shifts beneath it — even a small amount of movement is enough to throw off a door's alignment.

04
Moderate

Gaps between walls and ceiling, or baseboard and floor

This indicates separation, not just settling — the wall or baseboard is pulling away from a surface it used to sit flush against.

05
Moderate

Sloping or uneven floors

Try the marble test: set a marble or small ball on the floor. If it rolls on its own toward one side of a room, that's measurable foundation movement, not just an old, quirky house.

06
Moderate

Cabinets or countertops pulling away from the wall

Kitchen cabinets are usually installed dead-level and flush. A visible gap appearing months after installation is a strong, often-overlooked signal.

07
Higher concern

Stair-step cracks in exterior brick or mortar

These follow the mortar joints in a zig-zag pattern up the brick face. Unlike a drywall crack, this is happening in a rigid, load-bearing material — a stronger indicator than interior cracks alone.

08
Higher concern

Visible cracks in the foundation or slab itself

Especially horizontal cracks, or any crack wide enough to fit a coin into — this is the foundation itself showing the damage, not a downstream symptom of it.

09
Higher concern

Bowing or leaning walls

A wall that's visibly no longer plumb (vertical) indicates significant lateral pressure or settlement — this is well past the "monitor it" stage.

10
Higher concern

Sagging or visibly uneven roofline

If foundation movement is severe enough to show up at the roofline when viewed from the street, the problem has been progressing for a while — this warrants prompt attention.

How many signs is too many?

One or two early signs alone usually isn't an emergency. Three or more — especially mixing early and moderate signs — is when a free inspection stops being optional and starts being the cheapest next step available to you.
